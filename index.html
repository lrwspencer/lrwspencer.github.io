About Me

I am a 6th year PhD candidate in Economics at UCLA Anderson School of Management. My research focuses on Applied Microeconomics, Cultural Economics, and Economic History. I am particularly interested in the economic impacts of long-run changes in culture. I will be spending the 2025-26 year as a Visiting Fellow and guest lecturer with the Department of Economic History at the London School of Economics & Political Sciences.

Research

My work aims to address questions about the economic impacts of cultural change using archival and primary historical data.

Job Market Paper

Mind the Map: How the 1933 Tube Map Shaped London
This paper investigates the role of perceived proximity in shaping urban development, using the 1933 London Tube Map as a natural experiment. I show that areas appearing closer to central London on the map experienced more housing construction, higher prices, and greater mention in newspapers after 1933.
